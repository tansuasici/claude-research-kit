Claude Research Kit

Turns Claude Code from an eager intern into a rigorous co-author — source-grounded academic writing for LaTeX + BibTeX.

The cardinal rule: every claim traces to a real source. Never invent a citation, DOI, quote, or quantity. Never overclaim beyond the evidence.

The danger isn't bad prose — it's fluent prose

In code, a hallucination breaks the build and you find out. In a manuscript, a hallucinated citation looks correct — and survives to peer review, or print.

Without the kit

  • Invents a citation, DOI, or page number that looks real
  • States a statistic it doesn't actually have
  • "causes" where the evidence only licenses "is associated with"
  • Drifts off-thesis and pads with plausible filler

With the kit

  • Every \cite resolves in references.bib — enforced
  • Unknown numbers become [VALUE — verify], never a guess
  • Verbs calibrated to what the source licenses
  • Stays on the thesis; flags scope creep

One discipline, every time
